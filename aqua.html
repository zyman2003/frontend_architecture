Design

The Aqua Tower in Chicago stands as a marvel of contemporary architecture, renowned for its innovative and organic design. Completed in 2009, the tower was designed by architect Jeanne Gang of Studio Gang Architects. What sets Aqua apart is its undulating and rippling façade, resembling waves or contours of natural landscapes. This distinctive design is achieved through the strategic placement of balconies that create an irregular pattern, offering both aesthetic appeal and functional outdoor spaces for residents. Aqua incorporates sustainable features, such as energy-efficient systems and a green roof, showcasing a commitment to environmental consciousness.

History

Aqua was designed by Studio Gang Architects, led by firm principal and founder, Jeanne Gang, and it was the firm's first skyscraper project. When completed in 2009, the project was the largest ever awarded to an American firm headed by a woman.The Aqua Tower in Chicago represents a distinctive chapter in the city's architectural history, where it departured from conventional high-rise structures, challenging traditional norms with its fluid and undulating form.
